PORTFOLIO

Judging a book by it's cover. The challenge of designing a cover for Macbeth was that the material is so well known, almost everything has been done before. The challenge was to make it look fresh. Research and a knowledge of the material allowed me to find layers of meaning that I could translate into crisp, clean illustrations, finally landing on a design that respects the material while making it fresh and interesting.

Back to basics. The National @ the Knitting Factory poster is an entirely analog project. The challenge was to demonstrate a clear illustrative style through pen on paper.This was a very fun challenge that devoured multiple pens and markers in the pursuit of creating something unique and grungy.

Poster a Day Challenge It's important to challenge yourself as a designer. These posters are from a fourteen part series that is the result of a design-a-poster-a-day challenge.

Event Flyer: This project was to design an event flyer using advanced features in InDesign. I took a monochromatic approach and styled the table contents to include white space that made it more cohesive with the design.

Tri-fold Brochure: This project was to design a tri-fold info brochure for InDesign. Each term had to be visually demonstrated in a clean, cohesive style.

Hush, Listen to marginalized voices: The concept behind this sticker speaks to civil rights and what it means to be an ally. We live in a time where it is vital that marginalized voices get to tell their stories and share their experiences, and as allies, the best thing that we can do is listen. I modeled the “shushing” hand after the raised fist symbol used in civil rights movements. I felt that by using the same visual language, the context of the message would be more apparent.

Zen Typography: Creative constraints force us to be innovative in unexpected ways. This project or exercise is defined by constraints. Each iteration had a specific set of limitations on font style and size, forcing me to think of each element in a new way.
